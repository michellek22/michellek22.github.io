Easy Pancake Recipe

Pancakes are one of the worlds most beloved and versatile breakfast dishes. Soft, warm, and often served with a drizzle of syrup, a pat of butter, or a handful of fruits, pancakes have become a comfort food staple in many households. But did you know that pancakes have been around for thousands of years? The word “pancake” itself first appeared in English in the 15th century. Today, nearly every culture has its own version of the pancake. In France, it’s the thin and delicate crêpe; in Russia, the fluffy blini; in Ethiopia, the spongy injera; and in Japan, the thick and jiggly soufflé pancakes or savory okonomiyaki. Each variation reflects regional tastes and ingredients, making pancakes not just a breakfast dish, but a global culinary experience. Pancakes are easy to prepare, endlessly adaptable, and enjoyed by people of all ages. Whether topped with maple syrup, rolled with jam, or layered with savory fillings, pancakes offer a delicious and comforting way to bring people together around the table.

Perfect for Breakfast

Enjoy soft and fluffy pancakes with this recipe! Great for a quick and tasty breakfast.

List of Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until combined.
  4. Heat a pan over medium heat and lightly grease it.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the pan for each pancake.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook the other side.
  7. Serve warm with syrup and toppings of your choice!

Tips for Perfect Pancakes

For extra fluffy pancakes, do not overmix the batter. Let it rest for 5 minutes before cooking.
